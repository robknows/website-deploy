Ultimate Apple Pie in smaller steps, courtesy of BBC Good Food

Original recipe link 

=== Apples: 1kg apples, kitchen paper, large baking sheet ===
1. Layer of paper towels on large baking sheet
2. Quarter, core, peel and slice the apples about 5mm thick
3. Lay apples evenly on the baking sheet
4. Layer of paper towels over the apples on the baking sheet
5. Set aside for a bit


=== Pastry: 225g butter @ room temperature, 50g sugar, 2 eggs, 350g plain flour ===
6. Beat the butter and sugar in a large bowl until just mixed
7. Break in one whole egg and one egg yolk. Save the white of the second egg.
8. Beat the mixture until it looks a bit like scrambled egg.
9. Work in the flour with a wooden spoon, one third (of the flour) at a time.
a. When it starts to clump up, finish gathering it into a ball with your hands.
b. Wrap the dough ball in cling film
c. Put the clingfilmed dough ball in the fridge for 45 minutes.


=== Filling: 140g sugar, 1/2 tsp cinnamon, 3 tbsp flour, a large bowl ===
d. Mix the sugar, cinnamon and flour in a bowl large enough for the apples.


=== Tin: pie tin (20-22cm round, 4cm deep), kitchen paper ===
e. After the pastry has chilled, heat the oven to 190/170
f. Gently beat the egg white with a fork
g. Roll out 2/3 of the pastry, keeping the other 1/3 wrapped.
h. Use the rolled out pastry to line a pie tin leaving a slight overhang.
i. Roll the remaining 1/3 of pastry into a circle about 28cm in diameter
j. Pat the apples dry with kitchen paper
k. Briefly mix the apples into the bowl with the cinnamon/sugar mix
l. Tip the slightly mixed apple/cinammon/sugar stuff into the pastry-lined tin


=== Oven: a sharp knife ===
m. Brush some water around the pastry rim
n. Lay the pastry lid over the apples, pressing the edges together to seal
o. Trim the edges with a knife
p. Make 5 little slashes on top of the lid for steam to escape
q. Brush the pastry with the egg white and sprinkle with caster sugar
r. Bake for 40-45 minutes until golden
s. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes
t. Eat!
